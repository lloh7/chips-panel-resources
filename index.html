Building Arizona's Semiconductor Talent Pipeline

CHIPS Act National Context

  • $52.7 Billion Federal Investment: Historic commitment to reshoring semiconductor manufacturing and R&D capabilities
  • National Security Priority: Reducing dependence on overseas supply chains, currently 80% of advanced chips manufactured in Asia
  • Economic Impact: Each $1 federal investment generates $6 in economic activity across communities
  • Geographic Distribution: Major investments confirmed in Arizona, Texas, Ohio, New York, and other strategic locations
  • Workforce Challenge: Estimated need for 300,000+ new semiconductor workers nationally by 2030

Arizona's Replicable Model

  • Arizona's Success Formula: Industry partnership + state investment + registered apprenticeship = scalable workforce solution
  • TSMC Investment: $65B commitment creating template for industry-education collaboration nationwide
  • Multi-Source Funding Model: $4M ACA + $5M TSMC + federal CHIPS Act demonstrates sustainable financing approach
  • Scaling Opportunities: Rio Salado's apprenticeship framework ready for replication in other CHIPS Act regions
  • National Network Potential: ASA-SEMI partnership offers coordination mechanism for multi-state collaboration

Key Takeaways from Today's Panel

  • Arizona's Growing Demand: 47,023 current semiconductor workers projected to grow to 52,544 by 2034 (12% increase)
  • Apprenticeship Success: Rio Salado's TSMC partnership demonstrates effective industry-education collaboration
  • Multi-Source Funding: $4M ACA investment + $5M TSMC + federal CHIPS Act creates sustainable foundation
  • National Framework: ASA-SEMI partnership offers comprehensive solution requiring $2.35B federal investment
  • Immediate Opportunity: First-of-its-kind registered apprenticeship model ready for national scaling
